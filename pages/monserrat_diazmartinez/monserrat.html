My Internship Experience

This summer I interned at the Vaan Group, they are a company that does design and engineering of websites, mostly ecommerce, for various clients to optimize user experience.

My internship experience was fun. There were only two other workers in the office for most of the time since everyone else was working remotely, so I didn't get the typical big office experience like others might have but I still really enjoyed it for my first work experience.

I got to see a variety of aspects of a business; I attended client meetings and saw how to give presentations on designs and how important it is to give options, I saw what UX research is like for redesigning an e-commerce website and what kinds of feedback is used to better the site, I got a glimpse of what creating a new product brand is like and got to hear about different experiences—and recieve advice—that my coworkers had.

What I Worked On

I worked on various different things...

  • Taking the Shopify (an e-commerce platform) fundamentals courses in order to learn essential business knowledge as well as how to create an effective e-commerce site
    • I learned things such as...
      • project management methodologies
      • pricing strategies
      • sales strategies and how to manage risks
      • how to market yourself and your client
      • approaches for theme development of a Shopify site
      • an overview of Shopify's APIs used to customize the virtual storefront and its functionality
  • Doing market research to create a competitive landscape—a collection of information about realted brands, their products, and what makes them unique
  • Creating a mood board that contributed to strengthening brand image
  • Participating in interviews conducted to gauge how redesigns were going from a user's perspective
  • Attending meetings where designs were presented to clients and gave feedback
  • Learning Stripe—a way to collect payment information—to implement a button and form to collect donations
  • Contributing to a Wordpress website and discovering different plug-ins to achieve necessary look and function
  • Working on a Wirewax video
    • This is a platform that allows you to make a video shopable by having an overlay appear when a desired hotspot is clicked

Key Takeaways

There's a lot that I'm going to take away from this internship. I learned how many details are taken into account when working on a project. You need to have very clear communication with clients in order to give them what they want, and even then it could take several revisions before they are content. On that note, it's also important to give your opinion and stand your ground. You shouldn't have to lose your sanity just to please the client.

Another thing I learned was how many means there are to the same end. If you've already done something, or if someone else has done something, there's no need to remake the wheel; just pull out that old piece of work or download that plugin and tailor it to what you need instead of starting from scratch each time.

One last thing I learned is how easy it is to reach out to someone on the team for help. While it may have seemed intimidating to me at first, I quickly saw that even if you've never met, coworkers are available that have much more experience and therefore knowledge than you and I should take advantage of that. I also saw how valuable anyone can be to a project; when I went to meetings I was able to contribute and my opinions were actually respected and encouraged.
