About Me

I am an alumnus of the High School Of Fashion Industries. In our school we have three majors- fashion, business, and Graphic illustration or Art. I am an Art majored student and I really love to paint and craft. By Fall of 2018, I will be attending CUNY Hunter College.

I started attending ScriptEd during my junior year of high school with the foundations class afterschool. We learned HTML, CSS and a little Javascript. In the summer, I continued to take part in ScriptEd summer academy. I also took an advanced class at MLB. I am really thankful of the opportunity provided to us by ScriptEd and helping me acquire new skills.



My Internship Experience

My overall experience at RTR (Rent the Runway) was great and I loved it. Before, I am always afraid to think about pursuing a career in the Technical field. I thought computer programming will be a boring job where you sit in front of the computer all day long, tying codes and basically, you are married to your computer. I was thinking maybe I should be an artist, I would rather do something artsy every day then ruining my eyes in front of the computer.

But after coming to RTR, it is as if I had a different pair of lens looking at the industry. RTR made me see that computer programming can be very accomplished when you made something that everyone will like and use. It feels accomplishing when everything is going right and the bugs are fixed. During my internship, there was not one time that I felt bored. I remember Kiara (my internship partner) asking why do we have so much free food, drinks and even days like a scavenger hunt and company beach day? I really liked the answer that was provided which is you have to make your employees happy. I really saw that everyone in this company really enjoyed their job and get the greatest out of their job.

What I Worked On

Over the course of my internship, we learned two main languages used in this company which is React and Ruby. On our first few weeks of the internship, we worked in codecademy tutorials to learn about both languages. Meanwhile, we also started on our first ticket of the company, which was to show the warning message longer when something is removed so that the customer service agents are aware that the item is removed.

Our second ticket was basically styling a page. We fixed the spaces and made sure that the page was clear and everything was easy to find. We added the pagination function to the page and made sure that each section had about 5 transaction orders. We even renamed all of the sections because some might not know what all the abbreviations mean like RFF, PO, and etc.

Our last ticket was the Kustomer Contact page. We worked with the template provided by Kustomer and stylized and added the things that were needed in the wireframe.

Key Takeaways

I really had a fun experience at RTR. The key takeaway from this internship was that I was able to experience what it feels like to be on a team of software engineers. I was also able to obtain new skills, like new coding languages and problem-solving skills.

I also had a lot of fun during company events like Scavenger Hunt and Beach day. During these events I was able to know more about RTR, the team and meet new people in the company. I am also amazed when I visited the DFC because I was able to see how the clothes were packed, washed, and sent. But most importantly, we shadowed some customer service agents and we were able to see how they are using the things that we built.
