My Internship Experience

Interning at American Express has taught me that there are more important things than everyone liking or favoring you; your work and the things you contribute to the team speak more about your value.

Although I didn't feel 100% included in the American Express culture, I do feel as if my work will continue to assist software engineers at American Express. My co-intern and I struggled with being the youngest people on the team, and not having much in common with them. However, having to go through that difficulty made us closer, and I left American Express with a new friend and a new outlook.
A lesson from my manager that I will always carry with me is that my disadvantages (ie, my inexperience, age, etc) can be assets and valuable to my team. It allows me to look at things from a fresh perspective, and it also provides the team with an outsider's perspective.
To summarize, my time at American Express forced me to understand that I don't always have to go into a situation knowing everything, I just have to leave knowing something.

What I Worked On

My co-intern, Ashley, and I worked on implementing a React application called Storybook into the American Express software engineering community, starting with our particular team, Team 6. It's an application that allows you to render and see your UI components without needing to commit, push, or serve them through any particular app your company requires you to.

Over the course of five weeks, Ashley and I added all of the components used within a certain sector of the software engineering community at American Express, 'axp-base.' We also had to move our Storybook to the 'test' folder to allow our React Jest and Enzyme integrations to access tests used by our team's components.
Though it wasn't necessarily easy, I had a blast learning and familiarizing myself with new technologies, and different ways to compose an app from the ground up.

Key Takeaways

1. Though it sounds cliche, I think it's highly important to have fun, and to remember that we are all quite young, and that we are not expected to be super serious, unsmiling people. Remember that our internships are only for five weeks, and it's imperative that we make the most of it.

2. Learn! Was an internship really a success if a student left with nothing more than a few more contacts? Always ask clarifying questions, and even if you don't need to remmeber everything that your manager mentions, you should still have some sort of idea what they are talking about.
3. Storybook is an amazing, innovative application, and I truly believe that every software engineering company should use it. It's not only beneficial to companies that just desire an easier way to develop their UI components and apps, but also to companies that are transitioning to using React company-wide, not unlike American Express.
